Islamophobia

What is Islamophobia?

To put it simply, Islamophobia is the fear and hatred of Muslims. In the modern-day, it is the fear that is known to associate Muslims with terrorists due to the attack on 9/11. The worst Islamophobics discriminate against or keep away from Muslims because of the assumption that all Muslims are or can be terrorists. Islamophobia is, like other hate crimes, everywhere, in the media, on the streets, everywhere. Like other acts of hate, there may be violence, discrimination against the group, prejudice and stigmas.

When did the anti-Muslim attacks start?

It’s important to take note that anti-Muslim hate crimes have been around since before 9/11 (see statistics), in fact it has probably been around since the religion was founded. However, the anti-Muslim hate crime count spiked in 2001 after the attack on 9/11, and though the year after the numbers dropped, over the past couple of years the number of anti-Muslim hate crimes in the year has begun to rise.

Where does 9/11 fit in all this?

The attacks on 9/11 were lead by a terrorist group based in Afghanistan called “al-Qaeda”. The group is run by, you guessed it, Muslims. After the attack terror spread across the United States and the fact that such an attack was lead by a group of Muslims lead people to conclude that all Muslims must be associated with terrorists in some way, whether they are or are related to or have contact with terrorists.

What are the signs? How can I tell if an Islamophobic attack is happening?

Islamophobia has more or less the same characteristics as your average hate-driven attack except it is directed to Muslims and people who share traits that are typically associated with the stereotypical Muslim. Often verbal attacks involve calling Muslims out for being terrorists though they probably aren’t.

Modern Day Islamophobia

Here are a few examples of some events which happened as a result of Islamophobia

  • During his term President Donald Trump issued Executive Order 13769, also known as "Protecting the nation from Foreign Terrorist Entry into the United States". It prohibited the entry of refugees into the U.S. for 120 days and visitors from some Muslim countries could not enter for 90 days. Though there were certain exceptions (people with some bona fide relationship in the U.S. who could get a greencard) still received prejudice and discrimination from people working in the Transportation Security Administration and Customs & Border Control Officers
  • On March 15, 2019 a 28-year old Australian shot up two mosques in New Zealand. He also allegedly published a racist manifesto online prior to the attack in New Zealand
  • As for something a little closer to today, about a month ago(as of October 2019), at an anti-Islamophobia event a man allegedly attacked two Muslim women and some of the security at the venue with a knife, thankfully no one was harmed. Considering this was done in an anti-Islamophobia event it was probably an Islamophobic act. The police are still investigating at the moment

Fighting Islamophobia

There's quite a bit you can do to contribute to fighting Islamophobia, here is a list of some things you can do to help combat these hate-driven acts

  1. Support groups like CAIR who do what they can to combat Islamophobia, no need to join, just support
  2. Put positivity out in the media, unfortunately, today the media is a very easy way to spread ideas and allow them to grow or spread to the minds of others. We can use this to our advantage by putting out good content which embraces Muslims and portrays them as the non-terrorists that they probably are
  3. If you see something, say something and do something. If you see someone verbally abusing a Muslim or attacking them or calling them out as a terrorist, report it and if you think you can, intervene and defuse the situation. If someone has vandalized a Muslim’s property and called them a terrorist, report it and maybe help to clean up the mess.

These are among many ways to put up a fight against Islamophobia, we can all contribute different amounts so let's all pitch in what we can so that we can help
